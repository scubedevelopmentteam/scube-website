LinkedBridge Pro — Privacy Policy

Chrome Extension · Manifest V3 · LinkedIn to CRM · by SCube
Effective date: June 4, 2026 Last updated: June 8, 2026 Version: 3.0.0 Contact: scubedevelopmentteam@gmail.com
v3.0.0 — Multi-CRM Edition (Salesforce · HubSpot · Zoho · Dynamics 365 · Pipedrive)
✅ Short version: Your data never leaves your own browser except to go directly to the CRM you configure, using your own OAuth credentials or API key. We collect nothing. We have no servers. All LinkedIn data you extract goes only where you explicitly tell it to go.

1
Overview

SCube LinkedBridge Pro is a Chrome extension that reads publicly visible data from LinkedIn profile pages, company pages, search results, and posts — and lets you save that data as records in the CRM you connect. This policy explains exactly what data is accessed, where it goes, and what we never do with it.

Version 3.0.0 adds support for HubSpot, Zoho CRM (all regions), Microsoft Dynamics 365, and Pipedrive alongside the original Salesforce integration. The privacy model is identical for all CRMs: data flows directly from your browser to the CRM you configure — SCube operates no intermediate server.

Search Results (linkedin.com/search/results/*)

When you use bulk-queue mode on a search results page, the extension reads the visible result cards: name, headline, company, location, LinkedIn URL, and profile photo for each visible result. No background scraping occurs — only cards visible on screen at the time you trigger extraction.

Posts & Events (linkedin.com/feed/update/*, /posts/*, /events/*)

When you use the post-reactions or event-attendees feature, the extension reads the list of people who reacted to a post or RSVPd to an event: name, headline, LinkedIn URL, and profile photo. Only the data visible in the panel LinkedIn shows you as a logged-in member is read.

⚠️
LinkedIn Terms of Service: This extension reads publicly visible information displayed to you as a logged-in LinkedIn member. It does not automate scraping at scale, bypass authentication, or access data you could not manually see. You are responsible for ensuring your use complies with LinkedIn's User Agreement and applicable law.

4
Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect, transmit, or store any LinkedIn or CRM data on any server we operate
  • We do not collect analytics, usage metrics, crash reports, or telemetry of any kind
  • We do not collect your CRM credentials, OAuth tokens, or enrichment API keys — they remain on your device only
  • We do not share data with any third party except the CRM you configure and any enrichment provider you explicitly connect using your own API key
  • We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting
  • We do not operate user accounts, registration, or any server-side infrastructure
✅
The extraction count (extraction_count) stored in chrome.storage.sync tracks only a numeric total of profiles you have extracted — it syncs across your own Chrome devices via your Google account. It contains no profile data and is never sent to SCube.

Outbound Webhooks (Optional)

If you enable the optional webhook feature, the extension will POST a JSON payload to a URL you specify whenever a record is created or updated. The payload contains the same data that was sent to your CRM. SCube does not see or receive these webhook calls — they go directly from your browser to your configured endpoint.

8
Security

  • All communication with CRMs and enrichment providers uses HTTPS/TLS
  • OAuth tokens and API keys are stored only in chrome.storage.local, sandboxed to the extension and inaccessible to websites or other extensions
  • Dynamics 365 uses PKCE (Proof Key for Code Exchange) for its OAuth flow — no client secret is transmitted in the redirect
  • The extension uses Chrome Manifest V3, which enforces strict Content Security Policy and prohibits remote code execution
  • Webhook deliveries are signed with HMAC-SHA256 using a secret you configure, so your receiving endpoint can verify authenticity
  • No CRM credentials are hardcoded in the extension — you enter your own

To report a security vulnerability, email scubedevelopmentteam@gmail.com.
